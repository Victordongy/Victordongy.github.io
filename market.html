etf_weekly_returns <- function(ticker) {

The energy panel is of the worst performance, while its investment contribution is the most for the economy growth of the U.S. The reason is that no people have a good prospect for the traditional energy company. Considering that the operating income growth is -6%,-35%,17% in the three consecutive reporting year, the prospect is reasonable. However, this year, why the growth increases to 32% in the first quarter? I believe the big drive is the energy investment on the basis of infrastructure reconstruction since the presidency of Trump.

Another reason is that the oil price is poping since the start of 2017, due to the tense internaional regional uncertainty. The accumulated return rate of oil is nearly 100%, far above any aggregated stock market return rate since 2016. I believe the consistent increment of oil price as well as the uncertainty in the Arabian region would still drive the global oil price to a new peak.

The energy sector is fluctuating alawys with the oil price. Next year, the oil price will still recover from the 2014's level, for three reasons:

1) Demand recovers from the economic recover worldwide. Multiple super power, Eurozone, Japan, the U.S and of course China are expecting a more robost growth next year.

2) China's demand for energy leans to natural gas to replace the coal burning, the over-demand of natural gas will drive the global energy giants to deploy the related channel, equipment, and employees investment, which have been seen from third quarter financial report of the energy companies.

3) The political uncertainties in arabia regions as well as the financial stability in terms of the brand-new E-currency still lays out the doubt for the future oil price. Considering the high level of stock price in the States, RMB's out-of-expected going record high in the late December and Trump's new plan on infrastructure and internal trade. I believe investors should consider long oil and gold for commodities and also long for energy sector.

# A function that calculates the rolling correlation between a sector ETF and the SPY SP500 ETF. 

sector_index_correlation <- function(x, window) {
  
    merged_xts <- merge(x, etf_returns$'Index')

    merged_xts$rolling_test <- rollapply(merged_xts, window, 
                                         function(x) cor(x[,1], x[,2], use = "pairwise.complete.obs"), 
                                         by.column = FALSE)
    
    names(merged_xts) <- c("Sector Returns", "SPY Returns", "Sector/SPY Correlation")
    
    merged_xts
}

# Choose a sector ETF and a rolling window and pass them to the function we just build.
