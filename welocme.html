Some reflections on probability and distribution¶

The probabilitu theory has been faced with many arguments since its foundation at the very beginning day. The argument certer is if there is a predeterminant God who controls everything's distribution. However, the theory still walk through days and eras showing the most powerful isnghts of how everything works and goes.

One of my favorite distribution is beta distribution, because it somehow reflects the distribution of a certain probability, for example the batting average of a basesball player, or the three point shooting rate of a basketball player. The expression of beta distribution is as following:

$$ f(x) = \frac{\Gamma(\alpha + \beta)}{\Gamma(\alpha)\Gamma(\beta)}x^{\alpha-1}(1-x)^{\beta-1} $$

Suppose we know the Steve Curry, who is a very phenominal three point player has a career three point shooting rate of 42.1%, which is very terrific achievement. Then We might want to know what is his field goal in this season given in the first 10 game he have a 36 of 97, thus 37.1%. Then we could use the sample plot to describe the estimate of his whole season.

import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

%matplotlib inline

a = 42.1

y = stats.beta.pdf(x,a,b)
plt.plot(x,y)
plt.title('Original Beta(blue curve):a=%.1f,b=%.1f'%(a,b))
plt.xlabel('x')
plt.ylabel('Probability density')

y = stats.beta.pdf(x,a+36,b+61)
plt.plot(x,y)
plt.show

    The SFGK zero-intelligence model¶

    In this model due to Smith, Farmer, Gillemot and Krishnamurthy:

    • Limit orders can be placed at any integer price level $p$ where $-\infty < p < \infty$.

      • If worried about negative prices, think of these as being logarithms of the actual price.

    • Limit sell orders may be placed at any level greater than the best bid $B(t)$ at time $t$ and limit buy orders at any level less than the best offer $A(t)$.
