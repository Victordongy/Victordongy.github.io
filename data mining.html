KNN¶

A direct thought of mine is use clustering, one of the most common use of clustering is KNN, which profiles the nearest data point in the trained dataset

In [35]:
x_train, x_test,y_train,y_test = train_test_split(diabete.loc[:,diabete.columns !='Outcome'],
                                          diabete['Outcome'],stratify = diabete['Outcome'],random_state=66)
In [36]:
train_accuracy=[]
test_accuracy=[]
#try n_neighbors from 1 to 10
neighbors_settings = range(1,11)

for n_neighbors in neighbors_settings:
    #model building
    knn=KNeighborsClassifier(n_neighbors=n_neighbors)
    knn.fit(x_train,y_train)
    # train accuracy
    train_accuracy.append(knn.score(x_train,y_train))
    # test accuracy
    test_accuracy.append(knn.score(x_test,y_test))

plt.legend()

def plot_feature(model):
    plt.figure(figsize=(8,6))
    n_features=8
    plt.barh(range(n_features),model.feature_importances_,align='center')
    plt.yticks(np.arange(n_features),diabete.columns)

    • Limit orders can be placed at any integer price level $p$ where $-\infty < p < \infty$.

      • If worried about negative prices, think of these as being logarithms of the actual price.

    • Limit sell orders may be placed at any level greater than the best bid $B(t)$ at time $t$ and limit buy orders at any level less than the best offer $A(t)$.
